## Design Context

### Users
Placement agents, private-capital consultants, and business-development operators who hold valuable but incomplete contact lists in spreadsheets. They use the product during active fundraising, LP mapping, diligence, and CRM cleanup, often under time pressure and with a strong need to trust the provenance of every enriched field.

### Brand Personality
Refined, exacting, and quietly commercial. The interface should feel like a serious capital-markets workspace: fast to scan, restrained in tone, confident with data, and polished without becoming theatrical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Build a light, editorial workbench with dense information hierarchy, precise borders, calm surfaces, and selective high-signal color. Avoid generic AI-chat aesthetics, oversized marketing heroes, neon gradients, glassmorphism, and decorative card grids. The first viewport should be the tool itself.

### Design Principles
- Make the spreadsheet the center of gravity: every flow should lead toward cleaner rows, cited fields, and exportable outputs.
- Treat provider choice as an operator control, not a hidden implementation detail.
- Keep the interface credible for financial professionals: concise copy, compact controls, strong alignment, and visible audit trails.
- Prefer progressive disclosure over wizardry: simple defaults, deeper routing and cost controls nearby.
- Design for repeated use: stable tables, predictable controls, and exports that are always within reach.
