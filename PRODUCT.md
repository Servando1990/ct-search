# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Placement agents, private-capital consultants, business-development operators, and capital formation teams who hold valuable but incomplete contact lists in spreadsheets. They use Edna Search during active fundraising, LP mapping, diligence, and CRM cleanup, often under time pressure and with a strong need to trust the provenance of every enriched field.

The core job is to move from raw contacts or a natural-language research brief to cited, confidence-scored, exportable rows without losing control of provider choice, cost, or audit trail.

## Product Purpose

Edna Search is a Python-first research and enrichment workbench for capital formation teams. It lets operators upload CSV/XLSX contact lists or describe a natural-language search, route the job to the best provider by fit, cost, speed, confidence, or manual choice, then export cited results as CSV or PDF.

Success means users can trust the provenance of enriched data, compare provider tradeoffs quickly, and return to their spreadsheet or CRM with cleaner rows and fewer unanswered questions.

## Brand Personality

Sophisticated, simple, and chef-kiss. The interface should feel like a serious capital-markets workspace: fast to scan, restrained in tone, confident with data, and polished without becoming theatrical.

Voice and microcopy should be crisp, finance-native, operator-friendly, and direct. The product should feel like a trusted research analyst and table-first workbench rather than a generic AI chatbot.

Reference points:

- Linear for density, typography, crisp figures, and control ergonomics.
- Lightfield for minimal product language and a systems-minded approach to shared objects and primitives.
- Rogo for private-capital language, institutional trust, and finance-specific positioning.

## Anti-references

The public homepage is a product launch page. The first viewport should establish Edna Search as a credible product, show the core workflow visually, and create demand before asking the visitor to operate the tool.

The working product surface can appear as product proof, but it should not be exposed as raw internal tooling on the homepage.

Avoid generic AI-chat aesthetics, oversized SaaS heroes, neon gradients, glassmorphism, decorative card grids, noisy chrome, category-reflex finance palettes, and dark dashboards that feel designed for ambience instead of task work.

Do not hide provider choice, confidence, citations, warnings, or export affordances behind theatrical flows. The user should always know what data is being enriched, which provider is responsible, and what the run will cost.

## Design Principles

- Make the spreadsheet the center of gravity: every flow should lead toward cleaner rows, cited fields, and exportable outputs.
- Treat provider choice as an operator control, not a hidden implementation detail.
- Keep the interface credible for financial professionals: concise copy, compact controls, strong alignment, and visible audit trails.
- Prefer progressive disclosure over wizardry: simple defaults, deeper routing and cost controls nearby.
- Design for repeated use: stable tables, predictable controls, and exports that are always within reach.
- Keep it clean: remove ornamental UI before adding more chrome, and let typography, spacing, data, and interaction quality carry the polish.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Design to WCAG 2.2 AA by default. Controls must have visible keyboard focus, sufficient contrast, robust text overflow behavior, reduced-motion support, and status communication that does not depend on color alone.
